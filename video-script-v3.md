# MailSweep — single-take demo script (v3, Amit Agarwal style)

**Target runtime:** 4:30 – 5:00.
**Style:** continuous conversational narration. Single take, no segment breaks, no on-screen callouts. Talk through the product as you click — like Amit Agarwal's voice it demo.

**Tone:** warm, casual, helpful. Conversational connectives ("now back in the app", "let me show you", "as you can see"). Light enthusiasm at the cool moments ("smooth right?", "pretty handy"). Don't read like you're presenting a slide deck — read like you're walking a friend through the product on a Zoom call.

**Recording:** one continuous OBS take with your voice live, OR record voice on phone and screen separately and sync in Clipchamp. The script flows assuming you're doing both at once.

---

## Setup before recording

- Real Gmail account with cluttered inbox (10k+ unread, varied senders)
- MailSweep already installed (the demo starts at Marketplace and shows install, but you'll cut to your real account for the actual usage)
- Browser zoomed to 110%, no other tabs, bookmark bar hidden
- Phone on silent, AC down, quiet room
- Have the Marketplace listing URL ready in a second tab (after launch — for the install scene)

---

## The script — read straight through

> Read these in order, doing the actions as you go. Don't pause for "scenes." Just narrate naturally. The whole thing should flow like one breath.

---

**[Open with a cluttered Gmail inbox visible on screen, scrolling slowly.]**

> "Have you ever opened Gmail and felt completely overwhelmed by the thousands of unread emails just piling up? You've probably tried selecting fifty at a time and hitting delete. But that takes hours — and Gmail caps you at fifty per page anyway."

> "What if you could clean your entire inbox — or free up gigabytes of storage — in just a few clicks?"

> "Welcome to MailSweep — a Gmail add-on that bulk-cleans your inbox the way you've always wished Gmail would."

---

**[Cut to the Google Workspace Marketplace page for MailSweep.]**

> "Getting started is simple. Just go to the Google Workspace Marketplace, search for MailSweep, and install the add-on. When you install it, Google will ask for a few permissions — these are what let MailSweep work inside your Gmail. Just go ahead and authorize them."

**[Show the OAuth consent screen briefly, then approve.]**

> "And you're all set."

---

**[Cut to Gmail inbox with MailSweep icon now visible in the right sidebar.]**

> "Now let's open Gmail. You'll see the MailSweep icon in the right sidebar — just click it, and the app launches right there."

**[Click the MailSweep icon. Home card opens.]**

> "As you can see, MailSweep lives right inside Gmail. No separate app, no new account. Everything happens in this sidebar."

---

**[Hover slowly over the three preset sections.]**

> "MailSweep gives you three main things. It cleans your inbox by any filter you want. It helps you free up Gmail storage when you're running out of space. And it clears your unread badge — without deleting anything. Let me show you each one."

---

**[Scroll to the Quick Clean section.]**

> "First — the easy stuff. Quick Clean has one-tap presets for the categories that bloat every inbox. Promotions, Social, Updates, anything older than a year."

**[Click the Promotions preset row.]**

> "I'll clean out my Promotions. One tap. MailSweep uses Gmail's own search to find them — same query you'd type in the Gmail search bar — and shows me exactly how many emails it found. No surprises."

**[Preview card shows the count. Hold for a moment.]**

> "Three thousand four hundred emails. Let's move them to Trash."

**[Click Move to Trash. Confirm card appears.]**

> "MailSweep asks you to confirm — because we're about to do something big. And one important thing — these emails go to Gmail's Trash, not permanent deletion. Gmail keeps trashed mail for thirty days, so if you change your mind, you can restore them anytime, just like any other email."

**[Click Yes, move to Trash. Result card appears with green check.]**

> "And just like that — three thousand four hundred emails gone in seconds. Refresh Gmail and watch your inbox count drop. Pretty satisfying."

---

**[Back to home card. Type into the From field.]**

> "Now if the presets aren't enough, you can build your own filter. Sender, subject, label, or a date range — any combination. Let me clean every email from LinkedIn."

**[Click Preview matches. Preview shows count.]**

> "MailSweep shows me the exact count. But this is where MailSweep does something Gmail can't — review by sender."

**[Click Review by sender. Breakdown card opens with toggles.]**

> "It groups the matching emails by who actually sent them. So I can see these LinkedIn emails are split across job alerts, newsletter digests, group notifications, and reactions — each one's a separate sender."

**[Untick a couple of toggles slowly.]**

> "If I want to spare one of them — like, I do actually read the job alerts — I just untick it. The rest get trashed in one go. Then I confirm."

**[Click Trash selected senders. Confirm. Wait for result card.]**

> "Done. This sender breakdown is something I personally use a lot — it lets you clean aggressively without worrying about deleting something you actually want."

---

**[Back to home card. Scroll to Free Up Storage section.]**

> "Now — different problem. What if your Gmail isn't just cluttered, it's full? Google gives you fifteen gigabytes across Gmail, Drive, and Photos. When you hit that limit, your Gmail actually starts bouncing incoming mail."

> "MailSweep has a section for exactly this — Free Up Storage. Three presets: huge emails, big attachments, and anything that's old and heavy."

**[Click Big attachments. Preview card with size estimate.]**

> "Let me try big attachments. MailSweep estimates the storage you'll free — about three hundred and forty megabytes from this filter. That's a big chunk of your fifteen gigs."

**[Click Move to Trash. Confirm. Result card.]**

> "One thing to know — trashed mail still counts against your storage for thirty days, until Gmail auto-empties the Trash. If you want to free that space right now, MailSweep gives you a link to open your Gmail Trash so you can empty it immediately."

---

**[Back to home card. Scroll to Clear Unread Badge section.]**

> "Now the third thing. Sometimes you don't want to delete anything. You just want that red unread badge to go away. The forty-thousand-unread that's been mocking you for two years."

> "MailSweep can do that too. Clear Unread Badge — four presets for the most common cases. Old unread mail, unread Promotions, Social, Updates."

**[Click Old unread mail. Preview card shows unread count.]**

> "Notice the button is blue this time, not red. Because we're not deleting. We're just marking these as read. The emails stay in your inbox. Only the badge changes. And it's reversible — you can mark them unread again from Gmail anytime you want."

**[Click Mark as read. Confirm. Result card.]**

> "Done. Refresh Gmail and watch the unread count drop. Way more satisfying than scrolling through fifty pages clicking Mark as read."

---

**[Back to home card. Build a custom filter.]**

> "And one more thing. If you find yourself cleaning the same kind of mail over and over — same filter, every week — you can save it as a recurring sweep."

**[Click Save as recurring. The save-recipe card opens.]**

> "Give it a name. Pick how often — daily, weekly, or monthly. Optional: get a short email after each run so you know what was cleaned. That's off by default, because nobody wants more inbox bloat from a tool that's supposed to clean it."

**[Type a name, leave digest off, click Save recipe.]**

> "Save it, and MailSweep runs it automatically — daily, weekly, or monthly — in the background. While you sleep, your inbox cleans itself. Set it once, forget it. You can save up to twenty-five different recurring sweeps."

---

**[Back to home card. Cursor hovers on logo briefly.]**

> "And that's MailSweep. Quick to use, completely safe — everything happens inside your Gmail and Google's own infrastructure. Your emails are never copied to our servers, never read by anyone. The only thing MailSweep ever sees is your Gmail address — just to know whether your license is active."

> "MailSweep is a one-time five-dollar lifetime license. No subscriptions, no monthly fees, just five dollars and you have it forever."

> "Do visit our website for more details, and install MailSweep from the Google Workspace Marketplace today. Thank you for watching."

**[Hold final frame on home card for 2 seconds, then cut.]**

3. *"Everything happens inside your Gmail and Google's own infrastructure. Your emails are never copied to our servers, never read by anyone."* (Closing — covers the CASA exemption stance)

All three flow naturally inside the conversational pacing. No on-screen callouts needed.

## What's different from v2

- **No segment markers in the narration.** It reads as one flowing demo, like Amit's video.
- **Marketplace install scene at the start.** v2 skipped the install; this one walks the viewer through it like Amit does.
- **Conversational connectives throughout** ("Now back in the app", "And just like that", "Pretty satisfying").
- **The privacy/safety story moves to the closing**, woven in as a single conversational sentence instead of a labeled "Segment 10."
- **CTA is "visit our website and install" not "five dollars lifetime on the Marketplace"** — softer, matches Amit's style.

Use whichever feels right. v1 = 75s tight cut. v2 = 5min segmented. v3 = 5min single-take, conversational.
